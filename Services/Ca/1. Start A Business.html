Proprietorship Firm

Client Perspective: "I want to start a simple business with minimal investment and paperwork."

A sole proprietorship is a type of enterprise owned and run by one natural person with no legal distinction between the owner and the business entity. Perfect for individuals wishing to start a business with less investment.

Key Features:
  • Single ownership and control
  • Minimal compliance requirements
  • Easy to start and manage
  • Shops & Establishments Act, 1958 compliance

Partnership Firm

Client Perspective: "I want to start a business with partners to share risks and combine expertise."

A partnership firm is formed when two or more parties come together with a formal agreement to manage a business for profit. Risk and responsibilities are shared amongst partners, combining capital and expertise.

Key Features:
  • Shared risk and responsibility
  • Combined capital and expertise
  • Partnership deed registration
  • Indian Partnership Act, 1932 compliance

Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

Client Perspective: "I want the benefits of a company with the flexibility of a partnership."

LLP is an alternative corporate business form that gives the benefits of limited liability of a company and the flexibility of a partnership. It can continue its existence irrespective of changes in partners.

Key Features:
  • Limited liability protection
  • Separate legal entity
  • Flexible internal structure
  • Limited Liability Partnership Act, 2008

Private Limited Company

Client Perspective: "I want a professional business structure with growth potential and investor appeal."

A popular option for startups and businesses with higher growth aspirations. Provides a separate legal identity from its owners, safeguarding personal assets from business liabilities.

Key Features:
  • Separate legal entity
  • Limited liability protection
  • Professional structure
  • Companies Act, 2013 compliance

Public Limited Company

Client Perspective: "I want to raise capital from public investors and have a transparent corporate structure."

A limited company that can sell shares to investors, beneficial for raising capital. Requires minimum three directors with more stringent regulatory requirements compared to private companies.

Key Features:
  • Public shareholding capability
  • Enhanced transparency
  • Higher regulatory compliance
  • Minimum 7 members required

One Person Company (OPC)

Client Perspective: "I want to start a company alone but with limited liability protection."

Introduced through the Companies Act, 2013 to support entrepreneurs who can start a venture alone. Offers limited liability protection to the sole shareholder while being easy to incorporate.

Key Features:
  • Single member company
  • Limited liability protection
  • Easy incorporation process
  • Ideal for small entrepreneurs

Nidhi Company

Client Perspective: "I want to start a mutual benefit company for savings and lending among members."

A company that aims to increase the sustainability and saving habits of its members, to take deposits, and to lend only to its members for their mutual benefit.

Key Features:
  • Mutual benefit structure
  • Deposit and lending services
  • Individual members only
  • Central Government regulations

Producer Company

Client Perspective: "I want to form a company for farmers and agriculturalists to improve their standard of living."

A legally organized body of mainly farmers and agriculturalists, introduced to help raise the standard of living, income and profitability of Indian farmers.

Key Features:
  • Farmer-focused structure
  • Minimum 10 producers required
  • Minimum capital Rs. 5,00,000/-
  • Production and related activities

Foreign Company

Client Perspective: "I want to establish a business presence in India as a foreign entity."

An entity incorporated outside India but has a place of business in India or carries out business activity in India. Can be set up as an Indian company or as a foreign company.

Setup Options:
  • Wholly Owned Subsidiary
  • Joint Venture
  • Liaison Office
  • Branch Office

Non-Profit Organizations

Client Perspective: "I want to start an organization for charitable or social welfare purposes."

Includes Section 8 Companies, Societies, and Trusts registered for charitable objects such as promotion of arts, commerce, science, education, sports, protection of environment, religion, charity, etc.

Types:
  • Section 8 Company
  • Society Registration
  • Trust Registration
  • Charitable purposes
