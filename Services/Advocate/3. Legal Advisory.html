Legal Notice– Your First Step Towards Justice

Have a dispute and want to take legal action? Start with a legal notice. A legal notice is not just a piece of paper—it's a formal, strategic, and powerful communication tool. It signals your seriousness, puts the other party on alert, and often leads to resolution without the need for a full-fledged court case.

As your trusted legal partner, we help you draft and send legally compliant, clear, and professionally worded notices that make an impact—while protecting your rights and interests.

Why Legal Notice?

✅ To assert your legal rights without rushing to court

✅ To inform the opposite party about a legal obligation or breach

✅ To initiate a dialogue and possibly settle disputes amicably

✅ It often becomes a crucial piece of evidence in litigation

Our Legal Notice Process:

Consultation: Understanding your issue and reviewing relevant documents

Drafting: We prepare a professional, legally sound notice tailored to your case

Review & Approval: You get to review and approve the notice before dispatch

Dispatch: Sent via Registered Post or Courier with Proof of Delivery

Follow-up: Assistance in further legal action if there is no response

✅ Documents Required to Draft a Legal Notice:

To ensure accuracy and legal validity, we may require the following:

Copy of agreement/contract (if applicable)

Identity proof of the sender

Transaction proof (payment receipts, bills, etc.)

Communication history (emails, WhatsApp chats, letters)

Cheque and bank bounce memo (for cheque bounce matters)

Any supporting evidence relevant to the dispute

📌 Legal Value of a Legal Notice:

Acts as a warning: Gives the other party a fair chance to respond before litigation

Strengthens your case: Shows that you acted in good faith before approaching the court

Court admissibility: Can be used as documentary evidence to support your claims

Reduces litigation chances: Many matters get resolved after a strong legal notice is sent

🕒 Response Time and Legal Limitation:

Response Time: Generally, the recipient is given 7 to 15 days to reply

Time-sensitive: Notices under certain acts (e.g., NI Act 138) have strict deadlines, so don’t delay

Legal Limitation: Failing to send notice within prescribed time may weaken or forfeit your legal remedy
