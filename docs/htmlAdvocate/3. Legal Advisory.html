Legal Notice Your First Step Towards Justice

Have a dispute and want to take legal action? Start with a legal notice.
A legal notice is not just a piece of paperit's a formal, strategic, and powerful communication tool. It signals your seriousness, puts the other party on alert, and often leads to resolution without the need for a full-fledged court case.

As your trusted legal partner, we help you draft and send legally compliant, clear, and professionally worded notices that make an impactwhile protecting your rights and interests.

 

Why Legal Notice?

✅ To assert your legal rights without rushing to court

✅ To inform the opposite party about a legal obligation or breach

✅ To initiate a dialogue and possibly settle disputes amicably

✅ It often becomes a crucial piece of evidence in litigation

 

Types of Legal Notices We Handle:

  1. Civil Matters:
    • Property disputes
    • Loan defaults
    • Breach of contract
    • Partition claims
  2. Criminal Matters:
    • Defamation
    • Cheating (fraud cases)
    • Threats or harassment
  3. Consumer Cases:
    • Faulty services or defective products
    • Non-delivery or delay in delivery
    • Refund and compensation demands
  4. Cheque Bounce (under Section 138 of NI Act):
    • Timely and properly worded notices to protect your legal rights
  5. Employment/HR-related:
    • Termination disputes
    • Salary non-payment
    • Breach of employment agreement
  6. Corporate/Business Notices:
    • Vendor default
    • Non-payment by clients
    • Breach of MoUs, agreements

 

Our Legal Notice Process:

  1. Consultation: Understanding your issue and reviewing relevant documents
  2. Drafting: We prepare a professional, legally sound notice tailored to your case
  3. Review & Approval: You get to review and approve the notice before dispatch
  4. Dispatch: Sent via Registered Post or Courier with Proof of Delivery
  5. Follow-up: Assistance in further legal action if there is no response

 

✅ Documents Required to Draft a Legal Notice:

To ensure accuracy and legal validity, we may require the following:

  • Copy of agreement/contract (if applicable)
  • Identity proof of the sender
  • Transaction proof (payment receipts, bills, etc.)
  • Communication history (emails, WhatsApp chats, letters)
  • Cheque and bank bounce memo (for cheque bounce matters)
  • Any supporting evidence relevant to the dispute

 

📌 Legal Value of a Legal Notice:

  • Acts as a warning: Gives the other party a fair chance to respond before litigation
  • Strengthens your case: Shows that you acted in good faith before approaching the court
  • Court admissibility: Can be used as documentary evidence to support your claims
  • Reduces litigation chances: Many matters get resolved after a strong legal notice is sent

 

🕒 Response Time and Legal Limitation:

  • Response Time: Generally, the recipient is given 7 to 15 days to reply
  • Time-sensitive: Notices under certain acts (e.g., NI Act 138) have strict deadlines, so dont delay
  • Legal Limitation: Failing to send notice within prescribed time may weaken or forfeit your legal remedy

 

⚖️ Laws Under Which Legal Notices Are Commonly Issued:

  • Indian Contract Act, 1872
  • Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 (Section 138)
  • Consumer Protection Act, 2019
  • Code of Civil Procedure, 1908
  • Indian Penal Code, 1860 (for criminal notices)
  • Companies Act, 2013 (for corporate disputes)
  • Industrial Disputes Act (for employment disputes)
