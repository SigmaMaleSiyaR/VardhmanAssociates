✍️ What Is a Deed?

A deed is a written legal document that formalizes an agreement, right, or obligation between parties. It becomes a binding contract when it is properly drafted, executed, and (where applicable) registered.

Legal Importance of a Properly Drafted Deed

A legally valid deed ensures your agreement holds up in court.

It prevents disputes, fraud, and misinterpretation.

It defines the rights, duties, and liabilities of all parties clearly.

Deeds are often required for banking, tax filings, property registration, and court matters.

Risks of Poor or Self-Drafted Deeds

Missing legal clauses can make the deed unenforceable.

Unclear language can lead to disputes or legal battles.

Use of incorrect stamp paper, wrong jurisdiction, or unregistered documents can result in penalties or loss of rights.

DIY templates often fail to meet your specific legal needs.

⚖️ Stamp Duty & Registration

Stamp duty differs by state and type of deed (Sale, Lease, Gift, etc.).

Certain deeds like Sale Deeds, Lease Deeds over 11 months, Gift Deeds, etc. require compulsory registration.

We assist clients with:

Stamp duty calculation

E-stamping or physical stamp paper procurement

Registration process at Sub-Registrar office
