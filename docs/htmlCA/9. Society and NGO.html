What is an NGO?

An NGO (Non-Governmental Organization) is a non-profit group that operates independently of government and is formed to serve social, charitable, religious, educational, or humanitarian objectives. NGOs may be registered under different legal structures, each with its own benefits.

Main Objectives of NGOs & Societies

To serve the public without profit motive

Promote education, healthcare, and literacy

Work in rural development, women empowerment, and skill development

Provide aid during natural disasters

Promote environment conservation and animal welfare

Foster arts, culture, and social harmony

Key Objectives of NGOs & Societies

Most societies and NGOs are set up with one or more of these non-profit objectives:

Promotion of education, healthcare, or environmental protection

Conducting awareness programs, research, and training

Promoting arts, culture, literature

Providing relief to the poor or disaster-affected groups

Women empowerment, child welfare, animal protection
